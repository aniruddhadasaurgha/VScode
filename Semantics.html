Facts about semantics

Semantics is the study of meaning in language, encompassing how words, phrases, and sentences convey meaning. It involves understanding the relationships between signs and what they represent, including concepts like denotation (literal meaning) and connotation (implied meaning). Semantics plays a crucial role in linguistics, philosophy, and computer science, particularly in natural language processing and artificial intelligence.

Semantics is not just about words; it also includes how context, culture, and social factors influence meaning. For example, the same word can have different meanings in different contexts or cultures. Understanding semantics is essential for effective communication, as it helps us interpret and convey messages accurately.
